Alamir's Extended Grappling Rules

So you want to grapple?
From deep within Alamir's Palace, sages, alchemists and trainers have codified the rules for grappling. Only a few gnomes were hurt in the process.

Introduction

Players always try to grapple. They are creative and I don't want to limit their creativity. These extended rules are meant as optional for use with D&D 5th edition (TM) or a generic D20 system.

Rules

Unless otherwise indicated, the existing rules for grappling in DMG apply.

  • Grappling is an attack
  • Grappling is a contest between STR (Athletics) or DEX (Acrobatics)
  • Attacker and defender both choose which skill to use, they do not need to be the same
  • The difference between the two rolls indicates what happens.

Difference Result
0 No effect
1 to 3 Target is Restrained
4 to 7 Target is Restrained and/or Disarmed (attacker chooses)
8 to 14 Target is knocked Prone. Attacker may choose to follow target to the ground
15+ Target is Prone. Attacker may choose to inflict 1d4 + DEX or STR

There are special rules for the situation when either of the participates are holding a weapon.

If the attacker has no weapon, but the defender is armed, the defender gets an opportunity attack unless the attacker has advantage or the target is restrained or prone.

If the attacker is holding a weapon or shield and the defender is not, the attacker grapples with disadvantage.

If the defender is restrained, they can attempt to break free or use an ability that does not require the use of their hands.

If both the attacker and defender are holding weapons or shields, a clinch may result.

To break free, the restrained entity chooses either STR (Athletics) or DEX (Acrobatics) as does the entity doing the restraining. The roll is a contest. If the restrained entity's roll is higher, they break free.

The defender is restrained until they break free or until the attacker releases them.

While restraining, the defender can make an attack with a light weapon. Reloading is not possible.

If the attacker makes such an attack, the defender gets advantage to break free from restraint next round.

If the attacker knocks the defender prone, the attacker can decide to follow them down. If they do, the defender remains restrained on the ground. Otherwise they are not restrained - just prone.
